Mission : SAFETY - Alert urban blocks within area

Context

To protect population from danger.
Alerting is to inform inhabitants about an impending danger, allowing the population to shelter from it. The final goal is to minimize the number of victims and minimize subsequent evacuation time.
In SWORD, a population is alerted via the urban block it populates. An urban block cannot be alerted if it is not populated. An urban block has an "alerted" status (it is either "alerted" or "not alerted"). After being "alerted", the population may be "evacuated" faster.

Parameters

Mandatory

Danger direction

Expected enemy direction.

Limit 1

Right or left boundaries inside which the agent perform the mission.

Limit 2

Right or left boundaries inside which the agent perform the mission.

Area

The area containing the urban blocks to alert.

Optional

Phase lines

Lines used by units to adapt their behavior when they reach or cross them.

Rally point

Behavior
  • The commander agent issues a "Alert urban blocks" order to each capable agent. Every one of them is given a single urban block to alert (the closest one to them), and whenever a subordinate has finished to alert a urban block, another urban block to alert is given to him, for as long as there are urban blocks left to alert. When there are no longer such urban blocks, the subordinates are given a *Move To" order to the rally point instead (if there is no rally point, then the subordinates remain at their current position).
  • The other units receive a *Move To* order to the rally point (if there is no rally point, then they remain at their current position).

Specific cases

An urban block may not be alerted if:
  • The urban block cannot be inhabited (the report "urban block cannot be inhabited" is displayed)
  • The urban block is already alerted (the trace "the urban block is already alerted" is displayed)
In this case, the agent in charge of this urban block will pursue his mission with his other assigned urban blocks, and the commander mission continues.

Comments

End of mission

The mission ends when all the subordinate agents have reached the rally point (or, if there is no rally point, when they have finished to act on all the urban blocks).
